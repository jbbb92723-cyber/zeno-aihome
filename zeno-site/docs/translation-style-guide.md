# Zeno 英文翻译风格指南 / Translation Style Guide

## 1. Brand Voice in English

Zeno's English voice is:
- **Direct** — say what you mean, skip the filler
- **Grounded** — always connected to real experience, never theoretical fluff
- **Restrained** — confident without being loud; every sentence earns its place
- **Personal** — first person, honest, sometimes vulnerable
- **Anti-hype** — especially when writing about AI, renovation trends, or "life advice"

Think: a thoughtful practitioner writing to a smart friend. Not a marketer, not a professor, not an influencer.

## 2. Translation Principles

### Never do literal translation

Chinese and English have fundamentally different rhetorical structures. A sentence that reads naturally in Chinese often sounds stilted in English when translated word-for-word.

**Bad:** "Renovation is not just about how to renovate, but also about how people judge and how they grow."  
**Good:** "Renovation is the entry point. But judgment, trade-offs, and how you want to live — that's the real conversation."

### Transcreation over translation

Every English article should:
1. Preserve the **core insight** of the Chinese original
2. Restructure paragraphs for English reading rhythm (shorter, punchier)
3. Adjust cultural references (remove WeChat-specific context, add universal equivalents)
4. Use natural English idioms and sentence patterns
5. Cut repetition that works in Chinese but feels redundant in English

### Tone calibration

| Chinese tone | English equivalent |
|---|---|
| 朴素真实 | Understated, matter-of-fact |
| 克制表达 | Restrained, let the reader connect the dots |
| 经验之谈 | "I've seen this play out" — practitioner credibility |
| 不贩卖焦虑 | Never manufacture urgency; describe, don't prescribe |

## 4. Structural Guidelines

### Headings
- English headings should be concise and active
- Chinese: 装修预算为什么总超？ → English: "Why Renovation Budgets Always Overrun"
- Prefer statement over question form when the article answers the question

### Paragraphs
- Break long Chinese paragraphs into 2-3 shorter English ones
- Lead with the point, then support — reverse the Chinese pattern of building to a conclusion

### Lists
- Chinese articles often embed lists in prose; English should use explicit bullet points or numbered lists

### Cultural adaptation
- References to 公众号, 微信, 小红书 → either omit or generalize ("social platforms")
- CNY prices → keep as-is with "¥" or convert to approximate USD context
- Chinese names → keep pinyin, add brief context if needed

## 5. SEO for English Pages

- Every English page needs its own `<title>` and `<meta description>`
- Use `hreflang` tags: `zh-CN` for Chinese pages, `en` for English pages
- Canonical should point to the same-language version
- English titles should include natural keyword variations (e.g., "home renovation" not just "renovation")
- Alt text for images should be in English on English pages
